Appeal to Probability

An appeal to probability (or appeal to possibility) is the logical fallacy of taking something for granted because it would probably be the case (or might possibly be the case).

  1. Example 1 Premises:
    • I didn't bring my umbrella
    • Rain is possible
    • (unstated) Anything which is possible is certain
Example 1 Conclusion: It will rain Example 2 Premises: I bought a lottery ticket It is possible to win the lottery (unstated) Anything which is possible is certain Example 2 Conclusion: I will eventually win the lottery Wiki article

Swiss Chicken Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 6 boneless skinless chicken breast halves
    1 can cream of chicken soup
  • 6 slices Swiss cheese
  • 1 cup crushed Ritz crackers

Procedure:

  1. Preheat oven to 350. Place chicken in a greased 13x9-in. baking dish. pour soup over chicken. Top with cheese and sprinkle with crackers.
  2. Bake, uncovered, until a thermometer inserted in chicken reads 165, 35-40 minutes.
  3. Throw some crackers on it.
